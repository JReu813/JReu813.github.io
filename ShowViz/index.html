This is a snapshot of Broadway at the start of March 2020.

March 2020, Pre-Pandemic

Click on each of the selected theaters to learn more, and scroll forward when you are done.

On March 12th, former New York Governor Andrew Cuomo announced that no gatherings of over 500 people could happen in the state, citing Covid-19-related safety concerns.


By the evening, Broadway went dark.


Performances were supposed to come back a month later, but they did not. It took over a year for many shows to reopen, and, even then, some never did.

Fall and Winter 2021, Post-Lockdown

Interact with the slider to see when many shows turned their lights back on.


Despite the fact that live theater came back, it was no longer the same as what it was.

It was lights out on Broadway as we knew it, but lights up on a new era.

In the years past the shutdown, theater has had an audience to receive it: the vast majority of seats in Broadway theaters have been filled.
